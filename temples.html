Welcome to our exploration of temples! Come discover the beauty and meaning behind these special places. Let's unravel the cultural heritage of Pune together!

Omkareshwar Temple

Dedicated to Lord Shiva, Omkareshwar temple is one of the biggest temples in Pune. The temple is also among the most ancient temples you will find in the city as it was built under the Peshwa rule. Complete with intricate domes and carvings, the temple's architecture is one of the first things that will strike you and leave you in awe. Apart from the idol of Lord Shiva in the main temple, there are smaller temples as well which have idols of Lord Ganesha, Goddess Durga, Lord Shani, Lord Vishnu and Lord Hanuman. It is a 17th century Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in Shaniwar Peth. It was built by Chimaji Appa, the brother of Bajirao Peshwa, with the funds he obtained from liberating the Western Coast of India from the Portuguese. It also has the Samadhi of Chimaji Appa and his wife Annapurna, who committed sati after his death. It has a Nagara-style shikhar with intricate carvings of Hindu deities like Ganesha, Dattaguru, Vyasa, and others. It has a spacious veranda with ornamental columns in circular, polygonal, and square shapes. It survived the floods of Panshet in 1961, despite being situated next to the Mutha river. It was used as a meeting place and a taalim by freedom fighters like Lokmanya Tilak and Veer Savarkar during the Indian Freedom Movement.

Kasba Ganpati

Not only one of the most popular temples in Pune, but this one also has a historical story to go with it. Dedicated to the protector of the city – Kasba Ganpati, the temple was built by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj’s mother, Queen Jijabai. The idol which you will find inside the temple was founded by Shivaji Maharaj’s teacher. While the temple was visited by many, it was popularized after Lokmanya Tilak started celebrating festivals here during the freedom struggle. Since then, the temple has also become important for holding festivities related to Lord Ganesha. Do visit the temple on Ganesh Chaturthi to see the temple in all its glory. Kasba Ganpati Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha, located in Kasba Peth, Pune. It is one of the most popular and revered Ganesha temples in the city, and also one of the five main Ganpatis of Pune. It is considered as the local deity (gramadevata) of Pune, and has the privilege of leading the immersion procession on the last day of the Ganesh festival. It was a meeting place and a taalim for freedom fighters like Lokmanya Tilak and Veer Savarkar during the Indian Freedom Movement. It is a platform for socio-economic development and organizes various activities such as blood donation camps, health camps, and cultural programs.

Neelkantheshwar Temple

As the name suggests, this temple is dedicated to the Lord with the blue neck – Shiva. One of the most beautiful temples near Pune, Neelkanteshwar temple is situated atop a hillock, 900 metre above sea level. To reach the temple, you will have to trek for about an hour making your journey all the more worthwhile.While the structure of the temple resembles South Indian temples, it is the aura and serenity of the temple that draws visitors. From beautiful sunsets to views of the valleys, the location of the temple offers beautiful scenery worth making that steep climb.It was built by a local villager named Pundalik Kanhore in the 18th century, who found a Shivalinga on the hill and decided to construct a temple around it. It has more than 1500 statues of various Hindu gods and goddesses, as well as scenes from Hindu mythology, such as Ramayana, Mahabharata, and Samudra Manthan. It is a popular trekking destination, as it offers a scenic view of the surrounding valleys, rivers, and dams. The trek is about 2 km long and takes around 45 minutes to reach the temple from the base. It is also a place of worship and attracts devotees throughout the year, especially during Maha Shivaratri, when the temple is decorated with flowers and lights.

Lakshmi Narsimha Temple

Dedicated to Lord Narasimha, one of the avatars of Lord Vishnu, Shri Laxmi Narasimha Temple is among the most beautiful temples in Pune. Built in the Peshwa architectural style with black stones, the temple took 20 years to be completed and is located on the confluence of Bhima and Nira river. This location of the temple imparts it a surreal setting and elevates the entire aura of the temple. Apart from fortifying the temple against foreign invasions, the original idol of Lord Narasimha was replaced by a duplicate due to the fear of Mughal emperor Aurangzeb. All this makes for an interesting history of the temple. However, if you want to see glorious celebrations of any Hindu festival, visit it during Vaishakha Navratra to see processions like Dahi Handi. Lakshmi Narsimha Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Narasimha, an avatar of Vishnu, located in Pune district of Maharashtra. The temple is situated at the confluence of Bhima and Nira rivers, in Indapur taluka. The temple was built in 1787 in Peshwa architectural style, with a fortified west gate and a black stone idol of Narasimha. The temple also has several small shrines of other deities and a bell from a Portuguese church. The temple celebrates Vaisakha Navratri as its main festival. The temple is also a heritage site because of its association with the freedom fighter Vasudeo Balwant Phadke, who used to live there. The temple is believed to have a miraculous idol of Narasimha, which was brought from Panchvati by a Brahmin of Ratnagiri. Some devotees also claim to have seen the bricks of the pillar from which Narasimha emerged to kill Hiranyakashipu.

Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple

Dagadusheth Halwai Ganapati temple was built by a sweet seller named Dagadusheth Gadve. His guru suggested him to build the temple after his son died of a deadly disease. The temple’s architecture is quite unique because this is perhaps the only temple where you can see the idol of Lord Ganesha from outside. Not only the idol, but the inner sanctum is also visible from outside making it a perfect place to celebrate all the festivals with pomp and no restrictions. The 7.5 feet tall idol of Lord Ganesha is adorned with gold and precious gems which are offered by devotees from all over the world.The temple is a beautiful construction and boasts a rich history of over 100 years. Jay and Vijay, the two sentinels made of marble catch the eye of all at the outset. The construction is so simple that all the proceedings in the temple along with the beautiful Ganesh idol can be seen even from outside.The lighting of the temple during the Ganesh festival is marvelous. Shrimant Dagdusheth Ganpati Trust looks into the maintenance of the temple. The temple is situated in the centre of city, local shopping market is also the nearby temple. Various cultural activities like music concerts, bhajans, and Atharvasheersh recitation are organized by the trust.

Sri Balaji Mandir

Sri Balaji Mandir Pune is a close replica of the famous Balaji Temple of Tirumala, Tirupati. Therefore, people also call it Prati Balaji Mandir and Mini Balaji Mandir. It is built amidst lush green landscape, in a peaceful environment. And it is a treat for those who want to get the blessings of Lord Balaji. All pujas and sevas in this Balaji Mandir at Pune are performed by priests from the Tirupati Balaji Temple.Shree Balaji Mandir Pune is at Ketkawale, near Narayanpur. It is about 45 kilometres away from Pune. The road passes through green fields, gurgling streams and many small cascading waterfalls as you approach the temple. So, even the route to the temple is a memory worth savouring.The Tirupati Balaji Mandir near Pune has been constructed under the guidance of Tirumala Tirupati Temple Devasthanam.As you enter the premises, you'll come across the beautifully crafted entrance gate. It is made up of stone. The temple walls have carvings similar to the original temple. And the interior is decorated with woodwork.The black stone and other building materials used in the temple have been brought from Kanchipuram. And the main deity of the temple, Lord Balaji, is carved in wood. It is painted with grey oil paint. It took almost 7 years, from 1996 to 2003, to build this temple.

Parvati Hill Temple

As the name suggests, the temple is located atop a hill, 2100 feet above sea level. To reach this beautiful temple in Pune, you will have to climb 103 steps. However, all the exhaustion from such a long climb will fade away when you finally see the temple, its idols and of course, the scenic view of the surrounding area. It is said that at the same place where the present temple stands, there used to be a smaller temple (or a shrine) where Nana Saheb’s mother used to go and pray. She was suffering from a disease and she vowed to build a larger temple once she was cured. The temple was then built by Nana Saheb, the third Peshwa. Parvati Hill Pune is one of the oldest structures of the city. It has the ancient Devdeveshwar Temple which is over 250 years old. Here, you'll also find four more temples, dedicated to Kartikeya, Rama, Vishnu and Vitthal. The Parvati Hill Temple Complex is also a part of the Maratha history and legends surrounding the Peshwa rulers. And the museum here serves as a reminder of the Peshwa rule.

Pataleshwar Cave Temple

Situated on the Jangli Maharaj Road in Pune, Pataleshwar Cave Temple is enshrined by Lord Pataleshwar, the God of the Underworld, who is a form of Lord Shiva. Carved out of a single magnanimous rock, this spellbinding monolithic find is located right in the heart of the city and is a revered Hindu shrine which is thronged by hundreds of devotees and pilgrims on an everyday basis. Other than Lord Shiva, the temple is also dedicated to Nandi and has idols of other Hindu gods and goddesses as well including Lord Ram, Sita, Lakshmi, Lakshman, Ganesh etc. The construction of the temple was inspired by the majestic Elephanta Caves but it was left incomplete. For the same reason, the temple has no real entrance; the only major entrance is next to a banyan tree in the courtyard. The temple is maintained by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI). Thronged by a number of devotees and explorers, Pataleshwar Temple is of religious relevance to Hindus. With all those carvings and miniatures drawn on its wall, the centuries-old temple is a treat to the eyes.

ISKCON NVCC Temple (Katraj)

ISKCON temple Pune is a Gaudiya Vaishnavism temple dedicated to Lord Krishna and Radha. It is also known as the New Vedic Cultural Center (NVCC) and was inaugurated in 2013 by the then President of India, Pranab Mukherjee. ISKCON NVCC (New Vedic Cultural Center) is located in the less congested area of Kondhwa near Pune. ISKCON NVCC is a temple devoted to Lord Krishna and Goddess Radha. The temple has lavish green hills in the background and provides its visitors with a great ambience. Once you enter the prayer hall, you will be awe-struck by the stunning idols of Lord Krishna and Goddess Radha. The idols are elegantly embellished with beautiful outfits and colourful garlands. The ceilings of the dome and the walls of the temple are artistically adorned with colourful paintings. The temple houses activities like youth training programmes and Bhagvat Gita reading classes, women and family empowerment activities, etc. apart from being the residency of Radha, Krishna and Balaram.

Chaturshringi Temple

One of the revered temples of Pune, the Chaturshringi Mandir should be on your list of places to visit when you’re in Pune. Located on Senapati Bapat Road near Pune University in Gokhale Nagar, this temple has been the place for the devotees of Mahakali and Shri Chaturshringi for ages. The word “Chaturshringi” is derived from the word “Chattu” which means “Mountain with four peaks” and represents faith and power. The main temple also includes idols of Goddess Durga, Ashtavinayaka and Ganesha. It is said to have been built during the reign of the Maratha king Shivaji. The temple is looked after by Chattushringi Devasthan (Temple) trustees. Chattushringi (Chattu means four) is a mountain with four peaks. The Chattushringi temple is 90 feet (27 m) high and 125 feet (38 m) wide and is a symbol of power and faith. One has to climb more than 200 steps to reach the shrine of Goddess Chattushringi. The presiding deity of the temple is Goddess Chattushringi, also known as Goddess Ambareshwari. She is also considered as the presiding deity of the city of Pune. The temple is maintained by the Chattushringi Devasthan Trust. Every year a fair is held at the foothills on the eve of Navratri. Thousands of people gather to worship the Goddess Chattushringi.

Jejuri Khandoba Temple

The Khandoba Temple of Jejuri is a Hindu temple dedicated to the god Khandoba, located on a hill in the town of Jejuri, Maharashtra, India. It is one of the most prominent Hindu pilgrimage centres of Maharashtra.Jejuri's Khandoba is a Kuladaivata of many farming families, Brahmins and nomadic Dhangar tribe of the Maharashtra and Deccan region.According to legends and folklore, Khandoba was a human Avatar of Bhagawan Shiva; he used to live and rule the region from Jejuri-gad where the Mandir is now present. The Mandir is also known as Jejuri-gad. Khandoba killed the demon brothers Mani and Malla, when they harassed people. The worship of Khandoba began in the 12th to 13th century. It was built by Yadavas in 12th century. The Khandoba temple was rebuilt during the reign of the Peshwas.In 1737–1739, Chimaji Appa, brother of Peshwa Baji Rao I, gifted Portuguese church bells from Vasai to the temple. He and his Maratha soldiers took the bells from Portuguese churches as conquest memorabilia, after defeating them in the Battle of Vasai (1737). The central deity Khandoba, also known as Khanderav, Khanderay, Malhari-Martand and Malhar, is one of the most popular deities of Maharashtra. Khandoba is regarded as an avatar of the God Shiva. Khandoba is the Kuladevata (ancestral tutelary deity) of many people of Maharashtra.

Katraj Jain Temple

The Katraj Jain Temple is a beautiful and serene place of worship for the followers of Jainism. It is also known as Aagam Mandir because it has the inscriptions of the 48 Aagams, the sacred scriptures of Jainism, on its outer walls1. Here are some more facts about the temple: It is located on a hill between the Pune-Satara highway, at Katraj in Pune, Maharashtra. It was built in the late 20th century under the direction of Shri Daulasagarji. It has a 12-feet tall idol of Mahavir, the 24th and last Tirthankara of Jainism, made of a five-metal alloy called Panchadhatu. It also has an idol of Parshwanath, the 23rd Tirthankara, and a goddess named Padmavati, who is popular among the devotees. It has a Dharamshala with modern facilities and a Bhojanalaya that serves vegetarian meals to the visitors. It has a Jal Mandir surrounded by water and a Goshala (cow shelter) on its premises. It offers a panoramic view of the Pune city and the Katraj Lake from the top.

Bhimashankar Temple (Khed)

The Bhimashankar Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Shiva, located in Bhimashankar village, Taluka - Khed, Pune district, Maharashtra. It is one of the 12 Jyotirlingas in India, which are considered to be the most sacred abodes of Shiva. The temple was built in the 13th century by the Vishwakarma sculptors in the Nagara style of architecture. The temple was patronized by the Maratha rulers, especially Shivaji and Chimaji Appa, who made endowments and donations to the temple. The temple is associated with the legend of Bhima, the son of Kumbhakarna, who was killed by Shiva after a fierce battle. The sweat that poured forth from Shiva’s body after the fight is said to have formed the Bhima river. The temple has a Shiva lingam made of black stone, which is about one and a half feet in height and eight inches in diameter. The temple has a sabhamandap (assembly hall) and a shikhara (spire) that were added by Nana Phadnavis in the 18th century. The temple has a large bell that was gifted by Chimaji Appa after he defeated the Portuguese at the Vasai Fort. The temple is surrounded by a wildlife sanctuary that is home to various species of flora and fauna, including the Malabar giant squirrel. The temple celebrates Mahashivaratri as the main festival, which attracts thousands of devotees from all over the country. The temple also observes Shravan Mass and Adhik Mass as auspicious months for worship and rituals. The temple offers various poojas and sevas to the devotees, which can be booked online through its official website.

Ghoradeshwar Temple (Talegaon)

Ghoradeshwar Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located on a hill near Talegaon Dabhade, Pune. It is also known as Shelarwadi Caves or Ghorawadi Caves, as it has several caves with intricate carvings of Buddhist and Hindu deities. The temple is believed to be a place where Sant Tukaram, a 17th-century saint and poet, used to meditate. To reach the temple, one has to climb about 400 steps or take a route without steps. The temple offers a scenic view of the surroundings and is a popular spot for picnics, hiking, and trekking. The temple is open from sunrise to sunset and there is no entry fee. However, one has to pay for parking on the road. There are no food or tea stalls on the hill, but there are many options on the road.

Tulshibaug Ram Mandir (Budhwar Peth)

Tulshibaug Ram Mandir is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ram, located in the city of Pune, Maharashtra, India. The temple was built during the Peshwa era, after the battle of Panipat in 1761, under Naro Appaji Khire, a nobleman in the Peshwa court12. The temple has a beautiful spire (shikhar) and a large hall (sabhamandap) with intricate carvings of Ram, Sita, Laxman, Hanuman, Ganesh, and Parvati. The temple is also known for its collection of ornaments in gold, silver, pearls, and diamonds. The temple is open from 10:00 am to 9:00 pm and there is no entry fee. The temple is located in the Tulshibaug market area, which is a popular shopping destination for traditional and household items. The temple is about 35 km away from Talegaon Dabhade and can be reached by road or rail. Despite being located in a busy commercial area, the temple provides a peaceful environment for devotees to worship and seek the blessings of Lord Rama. It's a place where people can feel close to their faith and be part of the traditions of the region.

Sarasbaug Ganapati Mandir

Sarasbaug Ganpati Mandir is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha, located in Pune, Maharashtra. It was built in 1784 by Shrimant Sawai Madhavrao Peshwa, the fourth Peshwa of the Maratha Empire. The temple is situated on an island in the middle of a lake, which was created by Shrimant Nanasaheb Peshwa in 1750. The temple is also known as Tyatala Ganpati, meaning the Ganpati in the lake. The idol of Ganesha in the temple is called Siddhivinayak, meaning the one who grants wishes. The temple is a popular pilgrimage site for devotees of Ganesha, especially during the festival of Ganesh Chaturthi. The temple complex also has a museum, a garden, and a pond with aquatic animals. The temple was also used as a secret meeting place for the Peshwas and their commanders to plan their military strategies against the Nizam and the British Empire in the 18th and 19th centuries. The original idol of Ganesha in the temple was made of Kurud stone, but it was replaced twice, once in 1882 and the second time in 1990. The current idol is made of white marble and carved by Rajasthani artisans. The temple complex also has a museum, which displays thousands of images and forms of Ganesha from different regions and cultures. The museum was built in 1995.

Nageshwar Temple

Nageshwar Temple is a 700-year-old temple complex dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in Somwar Peth, Pune. It is one of the oldest temples of the city and has a rich history and architecture. The temple was built by the Yadavas in the 14th century and later renovated by the Peshwas in the 18th century. The temple has a stone sanctum with a shikhara (spire) and a wooden sabhamandap (assembly hall) with intricate carvings. The temple is surrounded by other shrines of various deities, such as Vishnu, Hanuman, and Dattatreya. The temple also has two deepstambhs (lamp pillars) in the courtyard. The temple is situated near Nagzari, a stream that was once believed to have healing powers. The temple is a protected monument by the State Archaeological Survey of India and has been recently restored by the Pune Municipal Corporation. The devotees believe that the water of the reservoir near the temple could cure Leprosy. Although the colossal structure has inscriptions dating back to ancient times, the temple has since gone through various renovations by the government. The temple celebrates Mahashivratri every year as its main festival while it also celebrates Hanuman Jayanti and Vinayaka Chaturti.

Dehu Gatha Temple

Dehu Gatha Temple is a temple dedicated to Sant Tukaram, a 17th century saint and poet of the Bhakti movement. The temple is located in Dehu, a neighbourhood in Pune, Maharashtra. The temple was built in 1995 and has a massive statue of Sant Tukaram at the entrance. The main attraction of the temple is that it has all the 4,000 verses (abhangs) composed by Sant Tukaram carved on the walls. The temple also has a museum that displays various images and forms of Lord Ganesha, the elephant-headed god and the patron deity of Sant Tukaram. The temple is situated on the bank of the Indrayani river, which is considered sacred by the devotees. The temple is a popular pilgrimage site, especially during the festival of Ganesh Chaturthi and the annual palanquin procession of Sant Tukaram from Dehu to Pandharpur. What attracts people the most are the carvings of all Gathas ie legends/storis of Sant Tukaram on the walls of the temple which are very easy to read. It is said that one must read at least 3 Gathas on visiting the temple. The temple is located on Indrayani River's bank. Also, there is a place on the river where these Gathas floats which should not be missed out.

BAPS Shri Swaminarayan Mandir

BAPS Shri Swaminarayan Mandir is a temple complex built by the BAPS Swaminarayan Sanstha, a Hindu denomination within the Swaminarayan Sampradaya. The temple is located in Narhe, Pune, and was inaugurated in 2016. The temple is made of hand-carved stone and has a statue of Swaminarayan at the entrance. The temple also has an Abhishek Mandapam, where visitors can perform ritual bathing of the deity, a museum of Ganesha images, and a vegetarian restaurant. The temple follows the Akshar-Purushottam doctrine, which teaches that Swaminarayan is the supreme God and Gunatitanand Swami is his ideal devotee. The Swaminarayan temple has a beautiful lawn around the main temple which is carved of red stone. The temple is known as it’s extremely well maintained and clean. There is a daily aarti that also takes place at the temple at different timings. The temple also has several basic guidelines that they require the devotees and visitors to follow in order to keep the temples respect and glory. Shri Mahalaxmi Mandir

Shri Mahalaxmi Mandir is a temple complex in Pune, Maharashtra, dedicated to the trinity of goddesses—Mahasaraswati, Mata Lakshmi, and Mahakali. The temple was built in 1984 by Shri Bansilal Ramnath Agarwal and Mata Shushila Devi Bansi Lal Agarwal, who were inspired by the Mahalaxmi temple in Kolhapur. The temple has a Dravidian style architecture, with a 55 feet high shikhar and a 54 feet long terrace. The main idols of the goddesses are made of ancient marble and are adorned with gold and silver ornaments. The temple also has a museum of Ganesha images, a garden, and a pond. The temple is a popular pilgrimage site, especially during the festivals of Navratri, Diwali, and Mogra Utsav. . The temple has three forms of the goddess. One of which is Mahasarwati who symbolises knowledge, Mata Laxmi who symbolises wealth and prosperity and Mahakali to discharge time from death. The main idol of the Goddess is made from ancient marble. During the mogra festival, the idols of these gods are decorated and filled with thousands of flowers. The other festivals widely celebrated here are Navratri, Holi, Brahma Utsav, Ram Navmi and Janmashtami.

Narayani Dham Temple

Narayani Dham Temple is a temple dedicated to Narayani Devi, also known as Rani Sati or Dadi, a legendary woman who sacrificed herself to protect her honor. The temple is located in Katraj, Pune, and was built in 2002. The temple has a white marble structure with three shikharas and a four-storeyed entrance. The temple also has shrines of other deities, such as Hanuman, Ganesha, Khatu Shyam Baba, Krishna and Radha. The temple is a popular pilgrimage site for the Rajasthani community and celebrates various festivals throughout the year. Shri Narayani Dham temple came into existence in 1983, located at Katraj in Pune. There are idols of deities like Lord Hanuman, Shri Shyam Baba Jot, Shiva Linga, Lord Ganesha, so on in this temple. However, Goddess Narayani is the presiding deity of the temple. The temple conducts several activities like free medical check-ups, social awareness camps, gaushalas, etc., that help society.

Someshwar Temple

Someshwar Temple is a 900-year-old temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in Pashan, Pune. It was built by the mother of Shivaji Maharaj, Jijabai, in the Hemadpanthi style of black stone. The temple has a self-originating (Swayambhu) Shivalinga, which is believed to have been revealed by a cow that sprinkled milk over a snake hole. The temple also has a wooden assembly hall with intricate carvings, a deepstambh (lamp pillar), and other shrines of Hanuman, Bhairavnath, and Ganesha. The temple is situated near Nagzari, a stream that was once considered to have healing powers. One of the ashta pradhans of the Shivaji’s court, Hanumante, contributed to the renovation of the temple whose samadhi is present in the temple’s north side. There is also a sabha mandap, whose ceilings have wonderful carvings built by prominent Mughal general Shahiste Khan as repentance. The temple is built in black stone in the Hemadpanti sculpture with a deep mala of approximately 40 feet. It is surrounded by Bhairavnath, Hanuman, and Ganesh temple.

Kanifnath Temple

Kanifnath Temple is a temple dedicated to Kanifnath, one of the nine saints of the Navnath Sampradaya, a Hindu sect. The temple is located in Bopgaon, near Saswad, Pune. The temple was built in the 14th century and has a unique feature of a small window-like entrance, through which only men can enter by crawling. Women are not allowed inside the sanctum sanctorum, where the idol of Kanifnath is placed. The temple is surrounded by scenic hills and offers a panoramic view of the area. The temple is a popular destination for pilgrims and trekkers. Kanifnath temple’s name comes from a Hindu saint, Kanhoba, also called Kanifnath, one of the Navnath Sampradaya’s Mahayogi. This temple’s view is pretty impressive, with morning prayers and music in the background, one of the most spectacular views in the morning.
