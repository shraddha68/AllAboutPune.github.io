Parks and Gardens!

Pu La Deshpande Garden (Okayama Friendship Garden):

Pu La Deshpande Garden, also known as Okayama Friendship Garden, is a beautiful Japanese-style garden located in Pune. Named after the renowned Marathi writer and humorist Pu La Deshpande, the garden is a serene and well-maintained public space.

  • History:

    The garden was inaugurated in 2005 and was designed as a symbol of friendship between Pune and Okayama in Japan.

  • Design and Architecture:

    The garden is designed in the traditional Japanese style, incorporating elements such as stone lanterns, pagodas, a Zengarden, and arched bridges. It features lush greenery, water bodies, and various species of plants and trees. The garden's design reflects the cultural exchange and friendship between Pune and Okayama.

  • Attractions:

    Okayama Friendship Pavilion: A central pavilion in the garden representing the friendship between Pune and Okayama. It often hosts cultural events and activities. Zen Garden: A carefully designed area with raked gravel and rocks, providing a peaceful and meditative space. Bamboo Grove: A section with bamboo plants, adding to the Japanese aesthetic. Kanji Wall: A wall adorned with Japanese characters, adding an authentic touch to the garden. Tea House: A traditional Japanese tea house where visitors can experience Japanese tea ceremonies during special events.

  • Activities:

    Visitors can take leisurely strolls, enjoy the scenic beauty, and participate in cultural events and festivals organized in the garden. The garden is a popular spot for photography enthusiasts due to its picturesque setting.

  • Events:

    Various cultural events, festivals, and workshops are organized in Pu La Deshpande Garden, celebrating the cultural exchange between India and Japan.

  • Visiting Hours:

    The garden is generally open to the public during daylight hours, and specific event timings may vary.

Saras Baug:

Saras Baug is a popular public garden located in the heart of Pune. It is a historic and well-maintained garden that attracts visitors for its lush greenery, scenic beauty, and cultural significance.

  • History:

    Saras Baug was originally a lake, known as Talyatla Ganpati, created by Peshwa Baji Rao I in 1784 to address the water shortage in the city. In 1969, the lake was filled to create a garden, and a Ganesh temple was built in the center.

  • Ganesh Temple:

    The garden is home to the Sarasbaug Ganpati Temple, dedicated to Lord Ganesha. The temple is situated on an elevated platform in the center of the garden. The idol of Lord Ganesha in the temple is one of the most revered in Pune and attracts devotees during festivals, especially Ganesh Chaturthi.

  • Design and Features:

    The garden is designed with well-manicured lawns, flowerbeds, and walking paths. A prominent feature is the artificial lake with a bridge leading to the Ganesh temple island. The garden also has an open-air stage for cultural events and performances.

  • Peshwa-era Bridge:

    The original Peshwa-era stone bridge that connects the garden to the mainland adds a historical touch to the site.

  • Visiting Hours:

    Saras Baug is generally open to the public throughout the week. It is advisable to check the specific visiting hours as they may vary.

  • Photography:

    The garden is a popular spot for photographers, offering scenic views of the lake, temple, and surrounding greenery.

Empress Garden:

Empress Garden, officially known as Shri. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Udyan, is a well-known botanical garden located in Pune.

  • History:

    The garden was established in 1838 during the British colonial era and was initially named after Queen Victoria, who was the Empress of India at that time.

  • Design and Features:

    The garden spans across 39 acres and is designed in the Victorian style, featuring well-manicured lawns, flowerbeds, and a variety of trees and plants. It is laid out in a geometric pattern with walking paths, making it an ideal place for leisurely strolls and picnics.

  • Floral Diversity:

    Empress Garden is home to a diverse collection of flora, including rare and exotic plants, flowers, and trees. There are separate sections for roses, orchids, and various other flowering plants.

  • Garden Layout:

    The garden is divided into various sections, each dedicated to specific types of plants, including a Bougainvillea section and a section for medicinal plants. A butterfly park within the garden attracts visitors interested in observing and learning about different butterfly species.

  • Fountains and Sculptures:

    The garden features fountains and sculptures that add to its aesthetic appeal. The musical fountain in the garden is a popular attraction, especially during special events and evenings.

  • Events and Exhibitions:

    Empress Garden hosts various events, flower shows, and exhibitions, attracting botanists, nature enthusiasts, and the general public. The garden is also a venue for cultural events and educational programs.

  • Children's Play Area:

    There is a dedicated play area for children with swings and other recreational facilities.

  • Visiting Hours:

    The garden is generally open to the public during daylight hours. Visitors are advised to check for any specific entry requirements or updated visiting hours.

Kamala Nehru Park

Kamala Nehru Park is a prominent public park located in the heart of Pune.

  • History:

    The park is named after Kamala Nehru, the wife of India's first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru. It was established in memory of Kamala Nehru, who was an active freedom fighter and social reformer.

  • Features:

    The park is known for its lush greenery, tranquil ambiance, and panoramic views of the city. It offers a serene environment for relaxation and leisure activities. One of the main attractions of the park is the viewpoint, which provides a breathtaking vista of Pune cityscape, especially during sunset.

  • Attractions:

    Apart from the scenic views, Kamala Nehru Park also features various amenities such as walking paths, seating areas, playgrounds for children, and lush gardens.

  • Accessibility:

    The park is easily accessible by road and is a popular spot for locals as well as tourists visiting Pune.

Peshwe Park

Located near Saras Baug, this park is dedicated to the memory of Nanasaheb Peshwa and offers a tranquil environment for visitors.

  • Historical Significance:

    The park is dedicated to the memory of Nanasaheb Peshwa, a prominent historical figure from the Peshwa dynasty.

  • Features:

    Peshwe Park features well-maintained gardens, pathways, and recreational areas. It is a popular spot for local residents and tourists to enjoy a peaceful environment.

  • Ganesh Temple:

    Like Saras Baug, Peshwe Park also houses a Ganesh temple, contributing to its cultural and religious significance.

  • Connected to Saras Baug:

    Peshwe Park is often visited in conjunction with Saras Baug as they are located in close proximity to each other.

  • Children's Play Area:

    Many parks in Pune include dedicated spaces for children, and Peshwe Park may have a children's play area.

Osho Teerth Park

Osho Teerth Park, located in Pune, India, is a serene and sprawling public park named after the renowned spiritual teacher Osho (formerly known as Bhagwan Shree Rajneesh). It is one of the many attractions in Pune that draws visitors seeking relaxation, meditation, and natural beauty.

  • Size and Layout:

    Osho Teerth Park covers a significant area, providing ample space for visitors to stroll, meditate, or simply relax amidst lush greenery. The park features well-maintained walking paths, landscaped gardens, and tranquil water bodies.

  • Spiritual Significance:

    The park is named after Osho, a spiritual guru who gained international recognition for his teachings on meditation, mindfulness, and personal growth. Osho's followers, known as sannyasins, often visit the park for meditation and contemplation.

  • Facilities:

    Osho Teerth Park offers various facilities to visitors, including meditation areas, yoga spaces, and seating arrangements for relaxation. Additionally, there may be facilities for wellness workshops, seminars, and other events focused on personal growth and spirituality.

  • Accessibility:

    The park is open to the public, and entry may require purchasing a pass or paying a nominal fee. Visitors can access the park by private vehicles or public transportation.

  • Environmental Conservation:

    Osho Teerth Park emphasizes environmental conservation and sustainability. The park's management may undertake initiatives such as water conservation, waste management, and preserving indigenous flora and fauna.

  • Visitor Experience:

    Visitors to Osho Teerth Park often describe it as a peaceful oasis within the bustling city of Pune. Many appreciate the serene atmosphere, well-maintained surroundings, and opportunities for introspection and meditation.

Bund Garden

Situated on the banks of the Mula-Mutha River, Bund Garden is a well-maintained garden with a jogging track and boat rides on the river. Bund Garden, officially known as Mahatma Gandhi Udyan, is a well-known garden and recreational area in Pune.

  • History:

    Bund Garden was constructed by Sir Jamsetjee Jeejeebhoy, a Parsi philanthropist, in the late 19th century. The purpose was to provide water to the poor in times of scarcity.

  • Design and Features:

    The garden is designed with well-maintained lawns, flowerbeds, and walking paths. It has a picturesque setting along the riverbank, making it a popular spot for locals and tourists.

  • Boating:

    One of the key attractions at Bund Garden is boating on the Mula-Mutha River. Visitors can enjoy paddle boat rides, providing a unique perspective of the surroundings.

  • Ganesh Temple:

    The garden houses a small Ganesh temple, adding a cultural and religious touch to the environment.

  • Jogging Track:

    For fitness enthusiasts, there is a jogging track within the garden, providing a scenic route for exercise.

  • Children's Play Area:

    Bund Garden typically includes a dedicated play area for children with swings and other recreational facilities.

  • Bridge:

    The garden is connected to the mainland by a bridge, which adds to the charm and accessibility of the area.
