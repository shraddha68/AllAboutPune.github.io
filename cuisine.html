Culinary Delights of Pune!

Street Food Delights!

Pune, a bustling city in Maharashtra, India, boasts a rich culinary heritage, including a vibrant street food scene. Here are some popular street foods you can find in Pune:
Hover the image to know more ;)

Vadapav

This iconic Maharashtrian snack consists of a deep-fried potato dumpling (vada) served inside a bread roll (pav) with various chutneys and sometimes fried green chili.

Misal Pav

Another famous Maharashtrian dish, misal pav is a spicy curry made from sprouted moth beans or matki, topped with farsan (crunchy snacks) and served with pav (bread).

Pav Bhaji

A favorite street food across India, pav bhaji is a spicy vegetable mash served with buttered bread rolls. It's a delightful and filling snack loved by people of all ages.

Kanda Bhaji

Also known as onion pakoras or onion fritters, kanda bhaji are deep-fried onion slices mixed with gram flour batter, seasoned with spices, and served with chutney.

Sabudana Vada

These crispy and savory fritters are made from soaked tapioca pearls mixed with mashed potatoes, peanuts, and spices. They're a popular snack during fasting periods and enjoyed throughout the year.

Bhutta (Grilled Corn)

During the monsoon and winter seasons, you'll find vendors selling grilled corn on the cob, known as bhutta, on the streets of Pune. It's often seasoned with salt, chili powder, and lime juice for a flavorful treat.

Kachori

Kachori is a deep-fried snack made from a crispy, flaky crust filled with a spicy lentil or dal mixture. It's often served with tangy tamarind chutney and a sprinkle of chaat masala.

Pohe

A quick and nutritious breakfast option, poha is flattened rice cooked with onions, mustard seeds, curry leaves, turmeric, and other spices. It's often served with a sprinkle of sev (crunchy chickpea noodles) and lime juice.

Pani Puri/Golgappa:

This quintessential street food is known by different names across India. It consists of hollow, crispy puris filled with a spicy and tangy mixture of tamarind water, chickpeas, potatoes, onions, and various chutneys.

Sev Puri:

Sev puri is a crispy and tangy chaat made with small puris topped with diced potatoes, onions, tomatoes, and a variety of chutneys. It's garnished generously with sev (crunchy chickpea noodles) and chopped cilantro.

Dahi Puri:

Similar to sev puri, dahi puri features small puris filled with a mixture of yogurt (dahi), potatoes, chickpeas, and chutneys, creating a delightful combination of flavors and textures.

Bhel Puri:

Bhel puri is a savory snack made with puffed rice mixed with diced vegetables like onions, tomatoes, and boiled potatoes. It's seasoned with tangy tamarind chutney, spicy green chutney, and various spices for a burst of flavor.

Ragda Pattice:

Ragda pattice consists of spicy potato patties (pattice) served with a flavorful curry made from dried white peas (ragda). It's garnished with chopped onions, tomatoes, cilantro, and various chutneys.

Dabeli:

Originally from Gujarat, dabeli has become a popular street food in Pune as well. It features a spicy potato filling sandwiched between pav (bread rolls), garnished with a sweet and tangy tamarind chutney, pomegranate seeds, and roasted peanuts.

Pattice:

Pattice are spicy potato patties coated in breadcrumbs and fried until crispy. They can be enjoyed plain or served inside a pav (bread roll) as a delicious and filling snack.

Sweet Tooth!

Pune, like many Indian cities, has a vibrant culinary scene with a variety of sweet treats to satisfy any sweet tooth. Some popular sweet delicacies enjoyed in Pune include:

Modak:

A traditional Maharashtrian sweet dumpling made from rice flour or wheat flour dough filled with a sweet mixture of coconut, jaggery, and sometimes nuts or dried fruits. It's often associated with festivals like Ganesh Chaturthi.

Shrikhand

A creamy and indulgent dessert made from strained yogurt (known as hung curd) mixed with sugar, cardamom, saffron, and sometimes nuts or fruits like mango. It's a popular sweet dish in Pune and across Maharashtra.

Puranpoli

Puran Poli is a sweet flatbread stuffed with a mixture of cooked lentils, jaggery, and spices. It's a favorite during festivals and special occasions.

Mastani

A rich dessert drink from Pune made by blending milkshake with flavors like mango or chocolate, topped with ice cream and dry fruits.

Sheera

Also known as Sooji Halwa, it's a popular sweet dish made from semolina, ghee, sugar, and flavored with cardamom and nuts. Enjoyed as a dessert or breakfast item.

Kharvas

A traditional Maharashtrian dessert made from colostrum milk, sweetened and flavored with cardamom or saffron, known for its delicate flavor.

Basundi

It is a traditional dessert from Pune, Maharashtra. It's a rich, creamy milk-based treat simmered with sugar, saffron, cardamom, and nuts. Served chilled, it's a popular sweet indulgence during festivals and celebrations.
